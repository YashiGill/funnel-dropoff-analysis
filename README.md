\# E-Commerce Funnel Drop-off Analysis



\## Business Problem

An e-commerce company is losing potential revenue because users are dropping off before completing a purchase. This project identifies \*\*where, who, and why\*\* users drop off — and recommends specific actions to improve conversion.



\## Key Findings

\- Only \*\*6.57%\*\* of users who view a product actually purchase it

\- The biggest drop happens at \*\*View → Cart\*\* (68% of viewers leave without adding to cart)

\- Users browse products priced at \*\*$13 on average\*\* but only purchase items priced at \*\*$5\*\* — price is a conversion barrier

\- Brand conversion varies wildly: top brand converts at \*\*54%\*\*, worst at \*\*0%\*\*

\- Early morning users (6am) convert \*\*40% better\*\* than peak daytime traffic — suggesting higher purchase intent



\## Business Recommendations

1\. Add price anchoring or EMI options on high-price product pages to reduce sticker shock

2\. Audit and either reposition or delist consistently zero-converting brands

3\. Schedule retargeting campaigns and push notifications for 6am–9am window

4\. Add exit-intent prompts at the cart stage to recover abandoning users



\## Tools Used

\- Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) — data cleaning and analysis

\- PostgreSQL — business queries and segmentation

\- Power BI — 3-page interactive dashboard (Funnel Overview, Brand Conversion, Price Analysis)
